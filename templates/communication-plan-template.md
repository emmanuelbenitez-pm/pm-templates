## ðŸ“Š Communication by Type

### Recurring Communications

#### Executive Updates

**Communication:** Executive Status Report  
**Audience:** Executive Sponsor, C-suite  
**Purpose:** High-level progress, major decisions, escalations  
**Frequency:** Bi-weekly  
**Owner:** Project Manager  
**Method:** Email + Optional 30-min meeting  
**Template:** [Executive Summary format]  

**Content Outline:**
- Overall status (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Key accomplishments (3-5 bullets)
- Top 3 risks/issues
- Decisions needed
- Budget/schedule status
- Next 2 weeks priorities

**Distribution List:**
- Executive Sponsor
- CFO (budget visibility)
- CTO (technical oversight)
- Business Owner

---

#### Team Standups

**Communication:** Daily Standup  
**Audience:** Core project team  
**Purpose:** Coordination, blocker identification  
**Frequency:** Daily (Mon-Fri)  
**Owner:** Scrum Master / PM  
**Method:** In-person or video call  
**Duration:** 15 minutes max  

**Agenda:**
- Round robin: What did you do? What will you do? Any blockers?
- Parking lot items for offline discussion
- Brief announcements

**Ground Rules:**
- Start on time (9:00 AM sharp)
- Stand if in-person (keeps it short)
- Focus on coordination, not problem-solving
- Take details offline
- No status reports to PM

---

#### Steering Committee

**Communication:** Monthly Steering Committee Meeting  
**Audience:** Steering committee members  
**Purpose:** Governance, major decisions, strategic alignment  
**Frequency:** Monthly (first Thursday)  
**Owner:** Project Manager  
**Method:** Formal meeting  
**Duration:** 90 minutes  

**Agenda Template:**
1. **Welcome & Objectives** (5 min)
2. **Executive Summary** (10 min) - Overall status
3. **Progress Review** (20 min) - Milestones, deliverables
4. **Budget & Schedule** (15 min) - Financial status, timeline
5. **Risk & Issue Review** (20 min) - Top risks, open issues
6. **Decisions Needed** (15 min) - Formal approvals
7. **Q&A** (10 min)
8. **Next Steps** (5 min)

**Pre-Meeting:**
- Send slide deck 48 hours in advance
- Highlight decisions needed
- Include backup materials as appendix

---

#### Sprint Reviews/Demos

**Communication:** Sprint Review  
**Audience:** Stakeholders, product owner, team  
**Purpose:** Demonstrate working software, gather feedback  
**Frequency:** End of each 2-week sprint  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Method:** Demo + discussion  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  

**Agenda:**
1. Sprint goals review
2. Live demo of completed features
3. Stakeholder feedback
4. Discussion of next sprint priorities
5. Q&A

---

### Ad-Hoc Communications

#### Crisis Communication

**Trigger:** Critical issue, major incident, security breach  
**Audience:** Varies by severity  
**Owner:** PM + Incident Commander  
**Method:** Multi-channel (email, call, Slack)  
**Timeline:** Immediate (within 1 hour)  

**Communication Levels:**

**Level 1 - Critical (Production Down):**
- **Immediate:** Executive sponsor, technical lead, business owner
- **Within 1 hour:** Steering committee, affected users
- **Updates:** Every 30 minutes until resolved
- **Post-Incident:** Full post-mortem report within 48 hours

**Level 2 - High (Major Delay/Risk):**
- **Within 4 hours:** Executive sponsor, steering committee
- **Within 24 hours:** Full stakeholder group
- **Updates:** Daily until resolved

**Level 3 - Medium (Significant but Manageable):**
- **Within 24 hours:** Executive sponsor
- **Next regular update:** Full stakeholder group

---

#### Change Notifications

**Trigger:** Approved change request affecting stakeholders  
**Audience:** Affected stakeholders  
**Owner:** Project Manager  
**Method:** Email + meeting if significant  
**Timeline:** Within 48 hours of approval  

**Content:**
- What's changing and why
- Impact on timeline/budget/scope
- What stakeholders need to do
- Who to contact with questions

## ðŸŽ“ Communication Best Practices

### Do's âœ…

1. **Tailor the Message** - Executives need summaries, team needs details
2. **Be Consistent** - Same day/time for recurring meetings
3. **Overcommunicate Early** - Better too much than surprise
4. **Use Multiple Channels** - Important info via email + meeting
5. **Follow Up** - Confirm understanding of critical items
6. **Document Decisions** - Written record prevents confusion
7. **Listen Actively** - Two-way communication, not broadcasts
8. **Be Transparent** - Share bad news early with solutions

### Don'ts âŒ

1. **Don't Surprise Stakeholders** - No bombshells in large meetings
2. **Don't Over-CC** - Respect people's inboxes
3. **Don't Assume Understanding** - Confirm comprehension
4. **Don't Skip Bad News** - Transparency builds trust
5. **Don't Communicate Only When Problems Arise** - Regular rhythm
6. **Don't Use Jargon** - Match audience's language
7. **Don't Forget to Listen** - Communication is two-way
8. **Don't Send Mixed Messages** - Consistency across channels

## ðŸš¨ Crisis Communication Plan

### Crisis Severity Levels

**Level 1 - CRITICAL:**
- Production system down
- Data breach / security incident
- Safety issue
- Major PR crisis

**Response:**
- Immediate notification to executive sponsor
- War room activated within 30 minutes
- Updates every 30 minutes
- All hands on deck

---

**Level 2 - HIGH:**
- Major milestone at risk
- Key team member departure
- Vendor failure
- Significant budget overrun

**Response:**
- Notification within 4 hours
- Daily updates until resolved
- Sponsor involved in mitigation

---

**Level 3 - MEDIUM:**
- Minor delays
- Scope change needed
- Resource constraints

**Response:**
- Notification within 24 hours
- Regular status updates
- PM manages with team
