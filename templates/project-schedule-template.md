## ğŸ’¡ Schedule Management Best Practices

### Do's âœ…

1. **Update Weekly** - Review and update schedule every week
2. **Track Critical Path** - Monitor critical tasks closely
3. **Manage Float** - Use slack time strategically
4. **Communicate Changes** - Notify stakeholders of delays immediately
5. **Document Assumptions** - Record all schedule assumptions
6. **Build in Buffers** - Add contingency for high-risk tasks
7. **Review Dependencies** - Check dependencies weekly
8. **Baseline Schedule** - Freeze baseline after approval

### Don'ts âŒ

1. **Don't Skip Updates** - Stale schedules are useless
2. **Don't Hide Delays** - Transparency builds trust
3. **Don't Over-Optimize** - Good enough is better than perfect
4. **Don't Ignore Float** - Use available slack
5. **Don't Fast-Track Recklessly** - Consider risks
6. **Don't Forget Resources** - Schedule needs people
7. **Don't Skip Dependencies** - Leads to rework
8. **Don't Change Baseline** - Only change with approval

## ğŸ“ Schedule Compression Techniques

### When Behind Schedule

#### Fast Tracking
**What:** Overlap tasks normally done in sequence  
**Example:** Start coding while design is being reviewed  
**Risk:** Rework if design changes  
**When to use:** Low-risk overlaps, late in project

#### Crashing
**What:** Add resources to shorten duration  
**Example:** Add another developer to backend work  
**Risk:** Higher cost, learning curve  
**When to use:** Critical path tasks, budget available

#### Scope Reduction
**What:** Remove non-critical features  
**Example:** Defer mobile app to Phase 2  
**Risk:** Stakeholder disappointment  
**When to use:** Severe delays, budget constraints
