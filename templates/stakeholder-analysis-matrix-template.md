## ğŸ¯ What is Stakeholder Analysis?

Stakeholder Analysis is the systematic identification, assessment, and planning for engagement with individuals or groups who can impact or be impacted by your project.

### Why It Matters

- **Political Navigation:** Understand power dynamics and influence patterns
- **Communication Planning:** Tailor messages to each stakeholder group
- **Risk Mitigation:** Identify potential resistance or support early
- **Resource Optimization:** Focus energy on high-impact stakeholders
- **Change Management:** Anticipate and address concerns proactively

## ğŸ“Š Stakeholder Identification

### Stakeholder Categories

#### Internal Stakeholders
- **Executive Sponsors:** Fund and champion the project
- **Project Team:** Deliver the work
- **Functional Leaders:** Impacted departments/teams
- **End Users:** Will use the solution
- **Support Teams:** IT, HR, Finance, Legal

#### External Stakeholders
- **Customers:** External users or clients
- **Vendors/Partners:** Third-party providers
- **Regulatory Bodies:** Compliance/audit authorities
- **Investors/Board:** Financial stakeholders
- **Community:** Public or community groups

## ğŸ’¡ Stakeholder Management Best Practices

### Do's âœ…

1. **Start Early** - Identify stakeholders before project kickoff
2. **Be Honest** - Don't sugarcoat problems
3. **Tailor Messages** - One size doesn't fit all
4. **Listen First** - Understand before persuading
5. **Manage Up** - Keep sponsors informed and engaged
6. **Build Coalitions** - Create network of supporters
7. **Document Everything** - Track all stakeholder interactions
8. **Update Regularly** - Stakeholder landscape changes
9. **Address Resistance** - Don't ignore skeptics/blockers
10. **Celebrate Wins** - Recognize stakeholder contributions

### Don'ts âŒ

1. **Don't Ignore Politics** - Politics are real, navigate them
2. **Don't Surprise Executives** - No surprises in meetings
3. **Don't Over-Communicate** - Respect their time
4. **Don't Assume Support** - Verify engagement constantly
5. **Don't Fight Alone** - Leverage sponsor for tough situations
6. **Don't Promise What You Can't Deliver** - Credibility is everything
7. **Don't Treat All Equally** - Prioritize high power/interest
8. **Don't Skip Difficult Conversations** - Address issues early
9. **Don't Forget End Users** - They make or break adoption
10. **Don't Stop After Go-Live** - Post-launch engagement matters

## ğŸ“ Stakeholder Review Process

### Monthly Stakeholder Review

**Agenda (30 minutes):**
1. Review attitude shifts (5 min)
   - Who moved from neutral to supporter?
   - Any new skeptics or blockers?

2. Power/Interest changes (5 min)
   - Has anyone's position or influence changed?
   - New stakeholders identified?

3. Engagement effectiveness (10 min)
   - Is our strategy working for each quadrant?
   - What's working? What's not?

4. Action items (10 min)
   - What needs to change in our approach?
   - Who needs more/less attention?

### Quarterly Deep Dive (1 hour)

1. Full stakeholder landscape review
2. Influence network mapping update
3. Coalition strength assessment
4. Communication effectiveness analysis
5. Engagement strategy adjustments
6. Lessons learned
